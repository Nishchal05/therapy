Cause

A fever is a temporary rise in body temperature, usually caused by an infection. Other causes of fever include heat exhaustion, certain inflammatory conditions such as rheumatoid arthritis, and cancerous tumors. Some medications, such as antibiotics and drugs used to treat high blood pressure, can also cause a fever.


If you have a fever, it’s important to monitor your symptoms and seek medical attention if necessary. Fevers generally go away within a few days, but if your fever is accompanied by other symptoms such as severe headache, chest pain, or difficulty breathing, you should seek medical attention immediately.

Symptoms

Fever is a common symptom that indicates the body's response to an underlying infection, inflammation, or other medical conditions. The hallmark of fever is an elevated body temperature. Normal body temperature varies slightly among individuals but is generally around 98.6°F (37°C). Fever is usually defined as a body temperature higher than 100.4°F (38°C). Here are common symptoms associated with fever:

  • Elevated Body Temperature:
  • Chills and Shivering:
  • Sweating:
  • Headache:
  • Muscle Aches and Weakness:
  • Loss of Appetite:
  • Dehydration:
  • Increased Heart Rate:
  • Flushing of the Skin:

Treatment

  • Stay Hydrated:

    • Drink plenty of fluids, such as water, herbal teas, and clear broths. Adequate hydration helps prevent dehydration, especially when the body is losing fluids through sweating during a fever.

  • Rest:

    • Get plenty of rest to allow the body to recover and redirect energy toward fighting off the underlying cause of the fever.

  • Temperature Control:

    • Use over-the-counter fever-reducing medications, such as acetaminophen (Tylenol) or ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin), as directed. These medications can help lower body temperature and alleviate associated symptoms.

  • Cool Compresses:

    • Applying cool compresses or using a damp cloth on the forehead and body can provide relief from discomfort associated with fever.

  • Comfortable Clothing:

    • Wear lightweight and comfortable clothing to help regulate body temperature
